I rebuilt my website to be read by AI, not just Google

By Md Shafaat Ali Choyon · builds & runs AI in production · Growth & health strategist · 5 min read

A year ago I rebuilt my own website around a question most sites still aren't asking: what happens when your reader isn't a person scrolling Google, but an AI writing the answer for them?

The click is disappearing

The numbers are stark. Something like 80% of Google searches now end without a single click. AI Overviews have cut clicks to the top result by roughly a third. ChatGPT alone fields hundreds of millions of questions a week. People increasingly get the answer, not the list of links — which means "rank #1" is quietly becoming the wrong goal.

Optimizing to be the source, not the result

So I built for the machine that answers. That meant structured data describing who I am and what I've done in a form models can parse; an llms.txt file that greets AI crawlers and tells them what matters; a robots file that welcomes them instead of blocking them; and prose written to be quotable — clear claims, one idea at a time, easy to lift into an answer.

The new goal isn't to win the search result. It's to be the sentence the AI uses when someone asks.

The dual-market angle nobody's pricing in

Here's where it gets interesting for anyone outside the US. English content dominates what these models learned, so being the clear, well-structured source in an underserved space — a market, a language, a niche the giants ignore — is wide-open ground. The same move that helps a personal site get cited helps a Bangladeshi brand become the answer in a category no one has claimed yet.

This isn't a trick — it's a discipline

None of this games anything. It's the same old lesson wearing new clothes: be clear, be structured, be genuinely useful, and make it easy to quote you. That worked for search engines. It works even harder for answer engines.

The short version

  • ~80% of searches now end with no click; AI answers instead of listing links.
  • Optimize to be cited: structured data, llms.txt, crawler-friendly, quotable prose.
  • Underserved markets and languages are open ground to become the answer.
  • It's not a hack — it's clarity and structure, rewarded harder than before.

When an AI answers a question in your field, whose words is it using — and could they be yours?
